Another product article for Nepal ðŸ™‚ - this time it is about fleece sweaters. Fleece sweaters are mid-layer. You have three layers:

  • Base Layer: On your skin
  • Mid Layer: Insulation
  • Outer Layer: Against wind / rain / snow

Meaning the fleece is not meant to be wind or water proof, but to keep you warm. I will use it for trekking. They don't insulate as well as down; I think I will use them on the hike. The down is more for when I don't move.

There are three ticknesses: 100, 200, 300. The material is Polyester. For size M this will be around 800g for 300 g/m², 540g for 200 g/m² and 236g for 100 g/m².

Polartec Thermal Pro-Fleece seems to be a common material, Nanuk is the version from Jack Wolfskin.

  • Jack Wolfskin: Westfjord, 122 EUR, Nanuk 300 Bipolar;
  • Mammut Innominata Advanced ML Jacket: 113 EUR, 200 Polartec Thermal Pro
  • Odlo Midlayer 1/2 Zip Snowbird: 41 EUR; 100 g/m² - I own this since years and had it in the moist trekking route "West Highland Way" and in the cold weather of GR-20. I also use it for running in winter. It is super comfortable and looks nice.
