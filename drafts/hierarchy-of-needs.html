The hierarchy of needs is a psychological theory proposed by Abraham Maslow in 1943. It suggests that human needs can be arranged in a hierarchy, with basic physiological needs at the bottom and self-actualization at the top. The hierarchy is often depicted as a pyramid, with the most fundamental needs at the base and higher-level needs building upon them.

Lets see which companies are in which part of the hierarchy.

Physiological needs

Water

  • Tap Water: Most drinking water is likely supplied as tap water by municipal waterworks. However, bottled water is also available at a premium. In Germany, tap water costs about 0.0025€/L while bottled water starts at 0.20 €/L. So 100x more. Please also note that municipal waterworks also take care of the sewage system and waste water treatment. Hence they offer more per unit of water sold than bottled water companies.
    • SWM Munich: 11,604 employees, revenue of €7 billion (2024) providing water to 1.5 million people. Probably more as it's likely not only Munich, but also the surrounding area. They also operate public baths, public transport, electricity, and telecommunications.
    • Berliner Wasserbetriebe: 4751 employees, revenue of €1.3 billion (2023) providing 211 million m³ of water to probably 3.5 million people (about 165 L/(day*person) or 0.006€/L in revenue).
  • Bottled Water: The bottled water market is dominated by a few large
    • Nestle (Perrier, S.Pellegrino)
    • Danone (Evian, Volvic, Aqua)
    • PepsiCo (Aquafina, LIFEWTR, SodaStream)
    • Coca-Cola (Dasani, Smartwater, Ciel, Bonaqua)

Food

  • Primary Food Production: Similar to water, there is the primary food production with agriculture, livestock, and fishing.
  • Food Processing: The food processing industry is dominated by a few large companies that control a significant portion of the market. These companies often have a wide range of products and brands, making it difficult for smaller competitors to gain a foothold.
    • Nestle
    • PepsiCo
    • JBS
    • Unilever
    • Kraft Heinz

Energy

Energy needs should be split into groups: Electricity and fossil fuels. Due to climate change we need to move away from fossil fuels and thus electrify heating and transportation. This means we will move from oil and gas to heat pumps. We will move from gasoline and diesel to electric vehicles. So lets focus on electricity.

  • Primary Electricity Production: Operating power plants to generate electricity. This includes fossil fuel, nuclear, and renewable energy sources.

    • Engie
