Some really great and short examples of illusions. If you want an explanation of them, I have added a link to the corresponding Wikipedia article.

<h2>Checker shadow illusion</h2>
[caption id="attachment_20311" align="aligncenter" width="300" caption="Checker shadow illusion"]<a href="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/checker-shadow-illusion.png"><img src="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/checker-shadow-illusion-300x232.png" alt="Checker shadow illusion" title="Checker shadow illusion" width="300" height="232" class="size-medium wp-image-20311" /></a>[/caption]
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Checker_shadow_illusion">Checker shadow illusion</a>: The pieces A and B are of the same color.

<h2>Ebbinghaus illusion</h2>
[caption id="attachment_20261" align="aligncenter" width="240" caption="Ebbinghaus illusion"]<a href="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/eigenhaus-illusion.png"><img src="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/eigenhaus-illusion.png" alt="Ebbinghaus illusion" title="Ebbinghaus illusion" width="240" height="148" class="size-full wp-image-20261" /></a>[/caption]
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ebbinghaus_illusion">Ebbinghaus illusion</a>:  the first central circle seems to be smaller than the second central circle although they are of identical size.

The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%BCller-Lyer_illusion">M&uuml;ller-Lyer illusion</a> is simmilar.


<h2>Fraser spiral illusion</h2>
[caption id="attachment_20201" align="aligncenter" width="512" caption="Fraser spiral illusion"]<a href="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/fraser-spiral-illusion.png"><img src="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/fraser-spiral-illusion.png" alt="Fraser spiral illusion" title="Fraser spiral illusion" width="512" height="514" class="size-full wp-image-20201" /></a>[/caption]
Although you think you see a spiral, there are only concentric circles. This illusion is knowen as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fraser_spiral_illusion">Fraser spiral illusion</a>.

<h2>Grid illiusion</h2>
[caption id="attachment_20221" align="aligncenter" width="320" caption="Grid illusion"]<a href="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/grid-illusion.png"><img src="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/grid-illusion.png" alt="Grid illusion" title="Grid illusion" width="320" height="320" class="size-full wp-image-20221" /></a>[/caption]
Dark dots seem to appear and disappear in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grid_illusion">Grid illusion</a>.

<h2>Jastow illusion</h2>
[caption id="attachment_20231" align="aligncenter" width="333" caption="Jastrow illusion"]<a href="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/jastow-illusion.png"><img src="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/jastow-illusion.png" alt="Jastrow illusion" title="Jastrow illusion" width="333" height="209" class="size-full wp-image-20231" /></a>[/caption]
In <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jastrow_illusion">Jastow illusion</a>, the two figures are identical, although the lower one appears to be larger.

<h2>Z&ouml;llner illusion</h2>
[caption id="attachment_20241" align="aligncenter" width="225" caption="Z&ouml;llner illusion"]<a href="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/zollner-illusion.png"><img src="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/zollner-illusion.png" alt="Z&ouml;llner illusion" title="Z&ouml;llner illusion" width="225" height="159" class="size-full wp-image-20241" /></a>[/caption]
In the figure of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Z%C3%B6llner_illusion">Z&ouml;llner illusion</a> the black lines seem to be unparallel, but in reality they are parallel.

<h2>Hering illusion</h2>
[caption id="attachment_20251" align="aligncenter" width="125" caption="Hering illusion"]<a href="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/hering-illusion.png"><img src="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/hering-illusion.png" alt="Hering illusion" title="Hering illusion" width="125" height="200" class="size-full wp-image-20251" /></a>[/caption]
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hering_illusion">Hering illusion</a>: Two straight and parallel lines look as if they were bowed outwards.

<h2>Spinning Dancer</h2>
This image is the only animated one in this post.
[caption id="attachment_20281" align="aligncenter" width="150" caption="Spinning Dancer"]<a href="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/spinning-dancer.gif"><img src="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/spinning-dancer.gif" alt="Spinning Dancer" title="Spinning Dancer" width="150" height="200" class="size-full wp-image-20281" /></a>[/caption]
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spinning_Dancer">Spinning Dancer</a>: If the foot touching the ground is perceived to be the left foot, the dancer appears to be spinning clockwise (if seen from above); if it is taken to be the right foot, then she appears to be spinning counterclockwise.

<h2>Caf&eacute; wall illusion</h2>
[caption id="attachment_20351" align="aligncenter" width="400" caption="Caf&eacute; wall illusion"]<a href="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/cafe-wall-illusion.png"><img src="http://martin-thoma.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/03/cafe-wall-illusion.png" alt="Caf&eacute; wall illusion" title="Caf&eacute; wall illusion" width="400" height="257" class="size-full wp-image-20351" /></a>[/caption]
